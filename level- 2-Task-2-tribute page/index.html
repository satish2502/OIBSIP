Ratan Tata - An Indian businessman, investor, philanthropist and former
chairman of Tata Sons

Who is Ratan Tata?

  • Ratan Tata was born in Bombay, now Mumbai, on 28 December 1937,and is the son of Naval Tata (born in Surat). His biological maternal grandmother had been the sister of Hirabai Tata, wife of group founder Jamshedji Tata. His biological grandfather, Hormusji Tata, had belonged to the broader Tata family; Ratan therefore was a Tata by birth. Parents Naval and Sonoo separated in 1948 when Ratan was 10, and he was subsequently raised by the widow of Sir Ratanji Tata, his grandmother, Navajbai Tata, who formally adopted him through the J. N. Petit Parsi Orphanage. He has a half-brother, Noel Tata (from Naval Tata's second marriage with Simone Tata), with whom he was raised. His first language is Gujarati.

  • Educational Background:

    He studied at the Campion School, Mumbai till the 8th class, followed by Cathedral and John Connon School, Mumbai and at Bishop Cotton School in Shimla, and, in 1955, graduated from Riverdale Country School in New York City. In 1959, he then received a degree in Architecture from Cornell University, and in 1975, attended the seven-week Advanced Management Program of Harvard Business School — an institution which he has since endowed.

  • Early Career:

    After completing his education, Ratan Tata began his career working for various Tata Group companies. He started on the shop floor of Tata Steel and gained hands-on experience in the operations of the company

  • Leadership Roles within the Tata Group:

    Over the years, Ratan Tata held various leadership positions within the Tata Group. He played a pivotal role in the development of several Tata companies, including Tata Motors, Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), Tata Tea (now Tata Consumer Products), Tata Steel, and Tata Chemicals.

  • Chairman of Tata Sons:

    In 1991, Ratan Tata was appointed as the Chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group. Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded its global presence and diversified into new sectors such as information technology, telecommunications, and automotive manufacturing.

Achievements

  • Chairman of Tata Group:

    • Ratan Tata served as the Chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group, for over two decades from 1991 to 2012. Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded its presence globally and diversified into various industries, including steel, automotive, information technology, telecommunications, and more.

  • Acquisition of Major Global Brands:

    • During his tenure, Tata Group made several significant acquisitions, such as the purchase of Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) from Ford in 2008. This acquisition transformed Tata Motors into a global automotive player.

  • Tata Nano:

    • Ratan Tata was instrumental in the development and launch of the Tata Nano, often referred to as the world's cheapest car. This initiative aimed to provide an affordable transportation option to many people in India.

  • Corporate Social Responsibility:

    • Ratan Tata emphasized corporate social responsibility (CSR) during his leadership. He encouraged Tata companies to engage in philanthropic activities and contribute to the community. Tata Trusts, which hold a significant portion of the shares of Tata Sons, is one of India's oldest and most respected philanthropic organizations

  • Support for Education and Healthcare:

    • Under his leadership, Tata Group made substantial investments in the fields of education and healthcare. Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) played a pivotal role in the development of India's IT and software services industry.

  • Global Expansion:

    • Ratan Tata led the Tata Group to expand its global presence by establishing a significant foothold in various countries, including the United Kingdom, the United States, and other parts of Asia.

  • Investment in Innovation:

    • Ratan Tata has shown a keen interest in promoting innovation and entrepreneurship in India. He launched the Tata Nano 1 lakh car contest to encourage innovation in the automotive sector, and Tata Group has supported various startups and innovations.
